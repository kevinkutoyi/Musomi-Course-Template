About this Course

Welcome to the course CIM 897: Competency Based Curriculum. This course is designed to provide you with knowledge, skills and attitudes on Competency Based Curriculum. The core concepts of the course include the philosophy and theories that inform the design of Competency Based Curriculum; principles and core competences for basic education in Kenya; Competency Based Assessment practices; teacher capacity development requirements for implementation of Competence Based Curriculum; challenges, global practices and research on Competency Based Education.

Course Content

Philosophy of competency based education; Purpose, principles of Competency Based Education: opportunity, excellence, diversity and inclusion, differentiated curriculum and learning, parental empowerment and engagement, Community Service Learning; Theories that inform Competency Based Education: Instructional design theory, Visible learning theory, Dewey’s social constructivism, Vygotsky’s social constructivist theory, Bruner’s social learning theory, Gardner’s Multiple intelligence theory, Piaget’s cognitive development theory, Erik Erikson’s theory of psychosocial development; Competency Based Curriculum framework for basic education in Kenya: Core competences: communication and collaboration, self-efficacy, critical thinking and problem solving, creativity and imagination, citizenship, digital literacy, learning to learn; characteristics and learning areas for different levels of basic education; 21st century skills; Pertinent and Contemporary issues in education: inclusiveness, differentiated learning, communities of practice, ICT integration in learning, health education, life skills education, value based education, education for sustainable development; Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and their relevance to education; designing a Competency Based Curriculum; Competency Based Assessment: definition, methods and principles: validity, reliability, fairness, flexibility, access; Formative assessment (assessment for learning): instruments for formative assessment: observation schedules, checklists, rating scales, rubrics, questionnaires, project method, journaling, portfolio, profiling, continuous assessment tests, homework, anecdotal records; Summative assessment (assessment of learning); Assessment as learning: self assessment, self correction, self monitoring, peer correction; Competences to be assessed in the learning process; Teacher pedagogical requirements for implementation of Competency Based Curriculum; challenges facing the implementation of competency based curriculum in Kenya; Competency Based Higher Education in Kenyan context: status, requirements and challenges; global practices and research on Competency Based Education: comparative analysis in developed countries and Africa, research gaps, futuristic perspectives.

Glossary

Curriculum: A programme of study.

 

Competency: A set of measurable abilities, skills, knowledge, and behaviour that an individual must possess to perform tasks or roles effectively in a particular context.

 

Competency-Based Curriculum (CBC): An outcome-based curriculum that focuses on students' ability to demonstrate mastery of specific knowledge, skills, attitudes and values.

 

Core Competences: Essential knowledge, skills, attitudes, behavior and abilities that learners require to function effectively in various contexts, particularly in education, the workplace and the society.

 

21st Century skills: A broad set of knowledge, skills, work habits, and character traits that are crucial for success in today’s world.

 

Competency Based Assessment: A mode of assessment that measures an individual's ability to apply specific skills, knowledge, and behaviors effectively in real-world.
